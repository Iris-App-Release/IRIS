# The Watcher (World)

## What it is

> *"A massive, unblinking eye suspended in an endless black void. It watches."*

The Watcher is IRIS's second world and its mood piece: a single gigantic,
photo-real human eyeball floating in pure black. There is no planet, no stars, no
icons ŌĆö just the eye, the void, and the parallax. Moving your head shifts your
view of the eye exactly as the Earth world shifts the globe, so the eye seems to
sit in real space behind the glass, quietly tracking you.

It is deliberately a **visual reskin**, not a new engine: it reuses the exact
same sphere ŌåÆ mesh ŌåÆ shader ŌåÆ texture pipeline as the Earth, with no new
animation, rendering, camera, or physics systems. Defined by
`Worlds/the_watcher/world.json`, rendered by the `Eye` class in
[[rendering-engine]].

## How it renders

The `Eye` class draws **one** textured sphere at `R = 2.6` ŌĆö the same radius as
the Earth surface, so it occupies the same screen footprint. The `eye.frag`
shader (GLSL 120) gives it its biological look:

- **Wrap-lit diffuse** so the unlit side reads as translucent flesh rather than
  pure black, but still falls into oppressive shadow.
- **Tangent-space normal mapping** (relief kept subtle) for fine vein and iris
  detail.
- **Wet cornea specular + Fresnel** ŌĆö sharp over the cornea, broader on the
  sclera, with a faint moist sheen at the limb.
- It outputs `alpha = 0` ŌĆö historically the anti-bloom mask (the bloom bright-pass
  skipped it); now **inert** since bloom was removed, but harmless.

**Eye tracking (active):** The sphere now rotates toward the viewer's detected
head position every frame, so the iris actively follows the user. The `Eye.update(dt, hx, hy)`
method receives `hx`/`hy` directly from [[head-tracking]] (the same five-tuple the
off-axis system uses). A lerp (`GAZE_LERP = 0.10`) smooths the transition so
movement feels intentional rather than reactive. Gaze is clamped to ┬▒15┬░ horizontal
and ┬▒10┬░ vertical ŌĆö the realistic limits for natural human eye rotation before the
look reads as forced. When `hx = +1` (viewer left), the sphere rotates ŌłÆ15┬░ in yaw
(iris moves left to track the viewer); `hy = +1` (viewer up) produces ŌłÆ10┬░ pitch.
This is a pure object transform and does **not** touch the camera, parallax, or
off-axis math. No second tracking pipeline is created ŌĆö the existing `hx`/`hy`
values from `face_tracker.py` are re-used directly.

**Gaze drift (base):** A near-imperceptible drift (Ōēż1.6┬░ yaw, Ōēż0.96┬░ pitch, slow
sinusoids) is retained and blended on top of the tracking gaze, so the eye retains
organic micro-motion even when the viewer is still.

## Eye tracking data flow

| Source | Transforms | Destination | Effect |
|--------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| [[head-tracking]] `hx`, `hy` | Clamped to ┬▒32┬░/┬▒26┬░ | Eye.update(dt, hx, hy) | Gaze target angle |
| (same) | Lerped @ 0.30/frame | Eye._gaze_yaw, _gaze_pitch | Smoothed gaze state |
| Gaze state + drift sinusoids | Combined `yaw`, `pitch` | Eye.draw() glRotatef() | Iris rotates toward viewer |
| Eye sphere rotation | Mesh transform | [[rendering-engine]] framebuffer | Visible eye movement |

The head `hx`/`hy` values are the same pre-smoothed 5-tuple already consumed by
[[off-axis-projection]] for parallax ŌĆö **no second tracking pipeline is created**.
Both the eye and the camera math read the same source data independently.
